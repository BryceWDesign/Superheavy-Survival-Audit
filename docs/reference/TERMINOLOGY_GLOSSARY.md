# Terminology Glossary

This glossary defines the terms used throughout the repository.

## Ambiguity score

A repository-defined quantity intended to summarize how difficult it may be to
distinguish or interpret a decay pathway or candidate chain under stated
assumptions. This is not a measured physical observable.

## Benchmark

A comparison target used to evaluate whether a repository output aligns with,
diverges from, or remains unresolved relative to literature expectations,
evaluated data, or internal consistency checks.

## Benchmark divergence

The gap between a repository output and a chosen reference expectation. A
divergence is not automatically a flaw; it may indicate a bad assumption, an
incomplete feature set, a mismatch in framing, or a region of genuine tension.

## Chain legibility

A repository-defined notion of how clearly a decay chain may be identifiable,
traceable, and distinguishable from nearby alternatives under the repository's
scoring logic.

## Curated data

Data that has been selected, transformed, normalized, or annotated for use in
the repository. Curated data may still come from upstream evaluated sources and
must retain provenance.

## Daughter chain

A sequence of descendant nuclides produced through decay from an upstream
candidate nuclide.

## Evaluated data

Data that has been assembled and assessed by an upstream authority or source
program rather than defined by this repository. Evaluated does not mean perfect
or complete; it means the repository did not originate the scientific value.

## Information gain ranker

A repository-defined scoring framework intended to prioritize measurements,
candidates, or routes according to how informative they may be under uncertainty,
model disagreement, decay legibility, and feasibility constraints.

## Measured quantity

A quantity that originates from experiment or evaluated records tied to
experiment. Measured does not mean uncertainty-free.

## Neighboring chain

A nearby isotopic or elemental sequence used for comparison context around a
target superheavy region.

## Observability

A repository-defined framing for how detectable, interpretable, or distinguishable
a candidate signal or chain may be under the repository's assumptions. This is
not a claim of guaranteed experimental visibility.

## Prior

A modeling assumption introduced before fitting, ranking, or updating with data.
A prior must be labeled clearly and must not be presented as direct evidence.

## Processed data

Data generated by transforming raw or imported records into repository-ready
formats, tables, or feature sets.

## Provenance

The chain of source and transformation information that explains where a record
came from, how it was changed, and what version of the repository produced it.

## Raw data

Data retained in a form intended to remain close to the upstream source material,
with minimal or no repository-driven transformation.

## Route-feasibility abstraction

A non-operational modeling layer that penalizes or ranks candidate pathways
according to bottleneck categories, constraint classes, and other high-level
factors. It is not a laboratory instruction set.

## Sensitivity analysis

A method for checking how strongly a result changes when inputs, parameters,
priors, or model terms are varied.

## Surrogate

A repository-defined stand-in quantity used because the desired quantity is not
directly measured or available in the required form. Surrogates can be useful,
but they must always be labeled.

## Uncertainty propagation

The carrying forward of uncertainty information from inputs through transformations
or modeled outputs when feasible.

## Upstream source

Any external database, publication, dataset, patent, or reference from which the
repository obtains facts, values, metadata, or contextual framing.

## Versioned snapshot

A fixed export of repository data or generated outputs tied to a specific release
or repository state so that later comparisons remain reproducible.
