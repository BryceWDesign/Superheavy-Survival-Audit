# Claim Boundary Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines the outer boundary of what this repository may claim.

The repository may ingest, normalize, compare, rank, visualize, and audit
nuclear-data-adjacent information and surrogate model outputs. It may discuss
uncertainty, detectability, relative route difficulty, observability, and
benchmark agreement. It may not present those activities as proof of physical
control, practical stabilization, or experimentally achieved synthesis.

## Allowed claim classes

The repository may support statements such as:

- a dataset contains a given evaluated value or uncertainty
- a normalized record was transformed in a specified way
- a surrogate score increases or decreases under a stated assumption
- a benchmark aligns or conflicts with a literature expectation
- a route-feasibility abstraction penalizes certain bottlenecks
- a decay-chain observability metric indicates more or less ambiguity under the
  repository's stated scoring rules
- a model is sensitive or insensitive to a given assumption

## Disallowed claim classes

The repository must not claim:

- that a specific superheavy nuclide can be stabilized in practice
- that any field mechanism shown here solves superheavy instability
- that any ranking output constitutes experimental proof
- that a route-feasibility score is an operational recipe
- that the repository demonstrates laboratory viability
- that repository-defined surrogate constructs are direct measurements
- that an uncertainty estimate removes the need for experiment

## Surrogates and heuristics

Any of the following must be labeled clearly when used:

- surrogate score
- prior
- heuristic
- proxy variable
- repository-defined coefficient
- transformed interpretation layer

Surrogate terms may be useful. They may not be disguised as measured facts.

## Language rules

Preferred wording:

- "under this scoring rule"
- "within this repository's assumptions"
- "benchmark agreement suggests"
- "this does not establish"
- "this result is sensitivity-limited"

Avoid wording such as:

- "proves"
- "shows definitively"
- "unlocks"
- "activates"
- "solves"
- "demonstrates stabilization"
- "reveals the hidden mechanism"

## Visual output rules

Figures, tables, and dashboards must avoid titles or labels that imply
stronger support than the underlying method provides.

Examples of safer labels:

- "surrogate agreement score"
- "observability ranking under stated assumptions"
- "route bottleneck penalty index"
- "benchmark divergence map"

## Review rule

If a statement could be read by a reasonable outsider as stronger than the
underlying evidence supports, the statement must be rewritten more narrowly.

## Default posture

When in doubt, the repository should make the smaller claim.
